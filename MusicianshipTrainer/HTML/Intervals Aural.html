Intervals Aural

All intervals will be played ascending only (going up).

Ways to spot a second:
  • The sound is the start of a scale.
  • The notes form the first two different pitches of the song Happy Birthday.
Ways to spot a third:
  • The sound is the start of a major triad/chord.
  • The notes form the first two pitches of the well-known tunes Michael Row the Boat Ashore, Kumbaya My Lord and Oh When The Saints!
If you are still unsure of the answer, wait until both notes are sounded together. If the notes clash, it will be a second. If the notes sound nicely together, it will be a third.

Summary

Practise singing a scale and a major chord daily to help you recognise these intervals!

You are allowed and encouraged to sing the interval you hear, to help you answer this question. On the day of your exam, answer 'Second' or 'Third' confidently even if you are not sure of your answer.
